Advances in Stem Cell Therapy

In recent human history, the study of cloning has been the dream of researchers the world over. In animal models we find that “cloning” otherwise known as parthenogenesis or asexual reproduction provides unique ways for organisms to adapt to environmental stressors.
This ability to regenerate life has been a number of solitary aquatic organisms and organisms with complex social structures such as bees and ants. Humans are currently experiencing impacts from the human created systems, which is why we see increased numbers of gene, and mutation related illnesses, such as kidney diseases, blood disorders, cancers, and other environmentally related diseases, seen in impacted individuals.
The human answer to regeneration lies within our ability to regenerate and our ability to heal. This ability comes from unipotent stem cells and multipotent stem cells. These cells are often found in the liver, skin, fingers, teeth, and bone marrow amongst other places. These cells are able to regenerate themselves. However what we seek are cells that are able to regenerate the entire body hence the need for pluripotent stem cells.
